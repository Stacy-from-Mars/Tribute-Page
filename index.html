Stan Lee

Creator of legendary Marvel comic book superheroes.

Stan Lee at the World Premiere of "Marvel's Captain America - Civil War" in 2016.

Here's a time line of Stan Lee's life:

  • 1922 - Stanley Martin Lieber was born on December 28 in Manhattan, New York City.
  • 1939 - With the help of his uncle Robbie Solomon, Lee became an assistant at the new Timely Comics division belonging to pulp magazine and comic-book publisher Martin Goodman.
  • 1942 - Lee entered the United States Army and served within the US as a member of the Signal Corps, repairing telegraph poles and other communications equipment and returned from his World War II military service in 1945.
  • 1947 - He married Joan Clayton Boocock, originally from Newcastle, England.
  • 1950 - Their daughter Joan Celia "J. C." Lee was born.
  • 1960 - Throughout the 1960s, Lee scripted, art-directed and edited most of Marvel's series, moderated the letters pages, wrote a monthly column called "Stan's Soapbox", and wrote endless promotional copy, often signing off with his trademark motto, "Excelsior!" (which is also the New York state motto).
  • 1961 - The first superheroes Lee and artist Jack Kirby created together were the Fantastic Four.
  • 1962 - With Steve Ditko they created Marvel's most successful character, Spider-Man. Again working with Kirby, Lee co-created the Hulk, Thor Iron Man and the X-Men.
  • 1964 - Stan Lee with Bill Everett created Daredevil.
  • 1966 - Lee and Kirby created the Inhumans and the Black Panther.
  • 1972 - Lee stopped writing monthly comic books to assume the role of publisher. His final issue of The Amazing Spider-Man was #110 and his last Fantastic Four was #125.
  • 2017 - Joan Boocock, his wife of 69 years, died of complications from a stroke. She was 95 years old.
  • 2018 - Lee died at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles, after being rushed there for a medical emergency earlier in the day. He was 95 years old.
